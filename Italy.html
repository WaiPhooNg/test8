Basic History 1

Image source : Unspalsh.com

A small agricultural village was growing on the west coast of Italy around the same time in the 8th century BCE. It established the city of Rome, which grew to become Ancient Rome, one of the world's major civilizations. The Roman Republic would emerge first, followed by the Roman Empire. Much of Europe and the Mediterranean would be under its control. Much of today's western civilisation, including philosophy, art, and law, would be shaped by the influence of Rome and Greek culture. The Roman Empire was split into two parts in 395 CE: the Western Roman Empire and the Eastern Roman Empire. The Western Empire, which lasted until 476 CE, included Italy.

Basic History 2

Italy became the birthplace of the Italian Renaissance in the 1400s. Artists such as Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo excelled during this time period.Much of Italy desired to become one country in the 1800s. Italy became a constitutional monarchy and a united, sovereign country in 1871.Benito Mussolini became Italy's dictator in 1922. He was the dictator of Italy, which he turned into a fascist regime. During World War II, he supported the Axis Powers of Germany and Japan. Mussolini was dismissed from power when they lost the war. Italy became a republic in 1946.
